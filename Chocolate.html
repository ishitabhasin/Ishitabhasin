Chocolate Bar Ratings - Python Project

Motivation:

Sales have been always considered to be the engine that allows a business to go for miles and miles. However, how useful is to reach far distances when the journey has left the engine drowned? This is what happens when the focus is mostly on top line, and not how profit is being made.

Business Questions:

  1. What is the relationship between cocoa solids percentage and rating?
  2. Which countries are the largest producers of chocolate?
  3. Which country produces the best chocolate?
  4. What have the average chocolate ratings been over the years?

Context

This is a project I did as part of my course in Data Analytics with Career Foundry.On this project I decided to use the Chocolate Bar Ratings dataset which contains expert ratings of over 1,700 individual chocolate bars, along with information on their regional origin, percentage of cocoa, the variety of chocolate bean used and where the beans were grown.

Limitations

  • This data only takes into account the ratings for the dark chocolates, so does not provide a whole picture of the chocolate industry.
  • There are more than 800 entries missing from the 'Bean Type' column, removing which can give us false results.
  • There are about 74 entries missing from the column 'Bean Origin', which can skew the results geographically.

    2. Linear Regression Analysis

    "Hypothesis: As the cocoa percentage of the bars increases, so does its rating"

  • There's a slight negative correlation between Cocoa Percent and Rating, i.e. chocolate bars with higher cocoa percentage tend to have lower ratings.
  • We can see from the plot that for chocolate bars containing over 90% cocoa, their ratings are mostly below 3.
  • In fig 2., we see that for chocolates with cocoa percentage between 70% & 90 % the ratings are in between 2-4.
  • We can conclude that our moel performed poorly, and therefore we are unable to say that the cocoa percentages of the bars influences or predicts their ratings.

    • 3. Time Series Analysis

    Through the time series analysis, we can forecast for the future based on the past values. The stationarity test tells us if the data is stationary (uniform mean and median and not affected by seasonality), which are very important to carry on the test.

    Conclusion and Findings

    • A majority of chocolate manufacturing companies are situated in the US, but the best beans come from Central and South America.
    • Upon conducting different kinds of analysis, we were not able to establish a relationship between the chocolate ratings and the percentage of Cocoa. This tells us that more factors are at play and needs further analysis.
    • The data is narrowly based on just plain dark chocolate (min 70% cocoa) with an aim of appreciating the flavors of the cacao when made into chocolate. The sample is too small (one bar from one batch), which is not a good representation.
    • If we want to take the Ratings as the target variable, knowing the statistics of variables such as Cocoa Percent, Company(if known) etc., would be helpful. However, this .will require more bivariate, or even multivariate analysis on the correlation between variables.
